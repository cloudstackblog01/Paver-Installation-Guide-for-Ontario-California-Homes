What Are Pavers and Why Do They Matter?

Pavers are flat construction units made from concrete, brick, natural stone, or porcelain. They’re used to create hardscaped surfaces like patios, garden paths, driveways, and pool surrounds. What makes pavers stand out is their strength, visual appeal, and ease of maintenance compared to poured concrete or asphalt.

Another major advantage is the design flexibility. With various colors, shapes, textures, and patterns available, pavers can complement any landscape style, from modern to rustic.

Your Paver Options—Built for Style and Strength

No two outdoor spaces are the same, which is why we offer a wide variety of paver materials to suit different needs, styles, and budgets.

  • Concrete Pavers: A popular choice for everything from large driveways to walkways and parking areas, concrete pavers are known for their durability and easy maintenance. Available in a wide range of colors, shapes, and sizes, they allow you to customize your space without stretching your budget.
  • Brick Pavers: If you’re aiming for a classic, rustic feel, brick pavers deliver timeless charm. Although they cost slightly more than concrete, they make up for it with visual appeal and easy repairs. Cracked bricks can be replaced individually, and regular cleaning with soapy water keeps them looking sharp.
  • Natural Stone Pavers: For those who want something distinctive and elegant, natural stone is a standout option. These pavers come in materials like travertine, slate, bluestone, and sandstone. They not only offer a unique look but also feature natural interlocking patterns that increase durability and structural stability.
  • Permeable Pavers – Designed to let water seep through the joints to manage stormwater and reduce runoff.

Benefits of Paver Installation for Your Property

  1. Durability: Pavers are designed to withstand foot traffic, heavy vehicles, and extreme weather conditions. Unlike concrete slabs that can crack over time, individual pavers shift slightly with the ground, reducing the likelihood of major damage.
  2. Easy Repairs: If a section becomes damaged or stained, there’s no need to tear up the entire area. You can simply replace the affected pavers—saving both time and money.
  3. Improved Curb Appeal: A well-designed paver surface immediately adds character and refinement to your property. Whether it’s a winding stone walkway or a classic herringbone driveway, the clean lines and neat finishes help your outdoor space stand out.
  4. Safety: Pavers provide better traction than concrete, even when wet. This reduces the risk of slips and falls, which is especially important near pools or on sloped driveways.

Our Paver Installation Process: What to Expect

Every successful paver installation starts with a plan. Here’s a look at our step-by-step approach:

Site Assessment and Consultation

We begin by visiting your property to understand your goals, assess the area, and offer guidance on paver selection and design. Whether you’re working with a sloped terrain or existing landscaping, we provide thoughtful recommendations to ensure a smooth installation.

Excavation and Base Preparation

The old surface is removed, and the ground is excavated to the appropriate depth. Then we install a compacted base layer of crushed stone or gravel, which provides stability and drainage.

Edge Restraints and Sand Bedding

Edge restraints are installed to keep the pavers in place over time. A layer of sand is spread and leveled over the compacted base to provide a smooth bedding surface.

Laying the Pavers

We arrange the pavers according to the chosen pattern. Precision and alignment are key during this step, especially when working with intricate designs.

Compaction and Joint Sand

Once in place, the surface is compacted to set the pavers firmly into the sand, which rests on a properly prepared subgrade. Then, we sweep polymeric sand into the joints to lock everything in place and reduce weed growth or shifting.

Final Touches and Cleanup

We inspect the entire installation to confirm quality and performance. Once approved, we clean the surface and walk you through the finished project.

Maintaining Your Paver Surface

Paver surfaces are relatively low-maintenance, but a few simple tasks will keep them looking their best:

  • Sweep regularly to remove debris
  • Hose down or pressure wash as needed
  • Reapply joint sand every few years
  • Seal the surface to protect against stains and UV fading

Following a seasonal maintenance routine helps extend the life and appearance of your investment.

FAQs About Paver Installation

How long does the installation take?

Are permits required?
Not always—but for larger projects like driveways, or those involving significant grading, we’ll advise if a permit is needed and can help with the paperwork.

What’s the cost range?

Do pavers get hot in the summer?
Some materials, like natural stone, stay cooler underfoot. We can guide you toward the right choice based on usage and sun exposure.
